How FID Works

DeepScan uses a four-signal ensemble combining deep learning, error level analysis, noise forensics, and frequency domain analysis to detect AI-generated or digitally manipulated images — no single method is relied upon alone.

Detection Pipeline

STEP 01

CNN Inference

A PyTorch Convolutional Neural Network processes the full image and outputs a raw manipulation probability score.

STEP 02

ELA Analysis

Error Level Analysis re-compresses the JPEG and highlights regions with inconsistent compression artifacts — common in edited images.

STEP 03

Noise Forensics

OpenCV detects unnatural Gaussian noise distributions that are characteristic of GAN-generated images.

STEP 04

DCT Frequency

Discrete Cosine Transform analysis reveals anomalous high-frequency patterns introduced by AI upsampling algorithms.

Training the CNN

The included CNN ships with random weights. To get real predictions, collect a labelled dataset (e.g. FaceForensics++, DFDC, or CASIA), run train_model.py, and set the environment variable MODEL_PATH=best_model.pt before starting the server. The ELA, noise, and frequency signals are training-free and work immediately.
